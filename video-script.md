# Video script: Allowed Space Notebook

Target length: 4:40 to 4:55.

## 0:00-0:25 - Hook

This is Allowed Space Notebook, an interactive marimo notebook for the
alphaXiv paper "Bootstrap Principle for the Spectrum and Scattering of
Strings." The goal is to make one core idea tangible: under visible bootstrap
assumptions, a broad string-amplitude deformation space can compress toward
the Veneziano amplitude.

## 0:25-1:10 - Paper problem

The paper asks whether string amplitudes can emerge from an analytically
solvable bootstrap problem rather than being assumed upfront. The notebook is
not a full proof reproduction. It is a runnable map of the logic: background
axioms, stronger Regge and level assumptions, a simplified deformation family,
and a conditional rigidity result.

## 1:10-2:05 - Assumption controls

Show the toggles for unitarity, crossing, analyticity, Regge control, level
truncation, and ultrasoftness. Then move the q, r, and w sliders. Explain:
background axioms define a legal space, but the stronger premises do most of
the compression. As they turn on, rigidity rises and allowed volume shrinks,
but Assumption Risk also rises.

## 2:05-3:05 - Visual exploration

Show the assumption-risk versus string-rigidity chart. The star marks the
current scenario. Then show the q/r heatmap. The white point is the
Veneziano-like corner, and the star follows the current controls. This makes
the paper's "three-parameter family versus isolated point" story visible.

## 3:05-3:45 - Residue-zero scaffold

Show the residue-zero plot. The vertical guide lines are the Veneziano-like
zero locations in the toy scaffold. Moving q, r, and w shifts or distorts the
deformed curve. The point is not to replace the analytic proof, but to let a
viewer feel why stronger zero and softness assumptions matter.

## 3:45-4:25 - Research-platform handoff

Show the generated claim draft, evidence note, assumption-risk critique, and
report appendix summary. This is the link back to the larger
`s-matrix-qg-lab` platform: the notebook does not stop at a plot; it produces
reviewable research objects with limitations attached.

## 4:25-4:50 - Supporting platform demo

Briefly show the local React/Vite platform as supporting context: dashboard,
evidence library, claim review, analytics, and Allowed Space Game Mode. Say
clearly that the competition submission is the molab notebook, while the full
platform is the larger research environment behind it.

## 4:50-5:00 - Close

The notebook's claim is deliberately conservative: not "string theory is
proven as the universe," but "conditional rigidity becomes understandable when
the assumptions, evidence, and risk are made interactive."

## Lines to avoid

- "This proves string theory is the only possible universe."
- "The notebook fully reproduces the paper."
- "The React app is the submission link."
